# Family AI Help

Family AI help on the AIssisted Consulting site is plain-English setup and privacy guidance for household AI questions.

The family path is for plain-English AI setup questions, privacy boundaries, everyday examples, and household use cases where human judgment stays in charge.

## Scope Caveat

Treat this content as guidance and inquiry support. It can help families ask better setup, privacy, and review questions, but it does not stand in for parents, caregivers, teachers, professionals, or the people affected by a decision.

AIssisted Consulting does not certify tools for children, schools, health, money, privacy, security, legal, or compliance situations. When a question affects those areas, families should slow down and get the right human help.

## What Families May Need Help With

- Understanding account settings and basic tool behavior.
- Deciding what personal information should stay out of AI tools.
- Drafting, organizing, summarizing, or planning low-risk household tasks.
- Talking through what a child, teen, parent, or caregiver should review before relying on an AI answer.
- Creating simple household guidelines for when AI is useful and when it is not.

## What Not To Share

Families should be careful with names, addresses, routines, account details, documents, health information, school information, financial details, legal questions, location details, private family situations, and anything that could expose another person without their consent.

Account settings and privacy controls can change. Families should review the tool, the account, the task, and the information before deciding what belongs in an AI system.

## Human Judgment Boundaries

AI can help draft, organize, summarize, and plan. People still need to make decisions, review sensitive information, check sources, and choose when to get professional help.

AI should not run the household, decide for family members, solve every school issue, make health or money decisions, or stand in for a responsible person.
