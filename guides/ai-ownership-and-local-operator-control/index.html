Small business guide

Keep control when AI enters the workflow.

Before AI touches intake, CRM records, follow-up, or reporting, decide who owns the customer context, where data may go, and which decisions still require a person.

Ownership check

The business should remain the source of truth.

Tools can help with the work. They should not quietly become the only place where customer history, operating rules, or approval decisions live.

Four questions

Define control before connecting another tool.

Who owns the context?

Customer history, service notes, pricing rules, and exceptions should remain accessible to the business instead of being trapped inside one vendor.

Where does the data go?

Name what enters an AI system, what stays out, how long records remain, and which connected tools receive the result.

Who approves the action?

Customer-facing messages, commitments, refunds, scheduling changes, and sensitive exceptions need a visible human review point.

Can the workflow move?

Keep exports, documentation, and ordinary handoffs available so a useful workflow can survive a model, vendor, or tool change.

Practical boundary

Automation should make responsibility clearer.

A useful system records what happened, surfaces exceptions, and keeps the next decision visible. Read the workflow automation guide for a practical way to map the handoff.
