# AVOS Content Tone Rules

Status: Draft 2026-05-03
Depends on: `docs/avos/source-truth.md`, `docs/avos/audience-map.md`, `docs/avos/prompt-panel.csv`, `docs/avos/sitemap-plan.md`, `docs/avos/aseo-files-plan.md`
Boundary: Local planning artifact only. This does not authorize implementation, publishing, live prompt testing, schema/feed changes, deployment, or client-facing claims.

## Purpose

These rules define how the AIssisted Consulting rebuild should sound before any page copy is written. The site should feel calmer, more helpful, and more protective than a standard AI sales page while still giving serious small-business owners a clear next step.

## Brand Voice

The voice should be:

- Plain-English.
- Local and human.
- Calm, not alarmist.
- Helpful before promotional.
- Practical and specific.
- Honest about uncertainty.
- Protective of privacy and human judgment.

The voice should not be:

- Hype-led.
- Enterprise buzzword heavy.
- Fear-based.
- Overly technical on first read.
- Pushy about buying.
- Dismissive of non-technical visitors.

## Core Message Pattern

Use this pattern for most sections:

1. Name the real-life problem.
2. Explain what a careful AI workflow can help with.
3. State the boundary or human decision point.
4. Offer a low-pressure next step.

Example structure:

> If missed calls and delayed follow-up are making the day harder to manage, start by mapping one workflow. AI may be useful for organizing intake, reminders, and summaries, but people should still own judgment, exceptions, and customer trust.

## Small-Business Language

Use phrases like:

- "Start with one workflow."
- "Practical AI help for the work that keeps slipping."
- "Owner visibility."
- "Intake, scheduling, follow-up, and reporting."
- "A workflow you can understand and control."
- "Human review stays in the process."
- "Make the next step clearer."
- "Reduce admin drag."
- "Keep control of customer relationships."

Avoid phrases like:

- "Replace your staff."
- "Automate your whole business."
- "Guaranteed booked jobs."
- "Never miss another lead."
- "Hands-free revenue."
- "AI employee."
- "Set it and forget it."
- "Explosive growth."

## Family And Household Language

Use phrases like:

- "Plain-English AI guidance for families."
- "Start with safety and privacy."
- "Know what not to share."
- "Household help with human judgment in charge."
- "Setup questions, boundaries, and everyday examples."
- "Useful for drafting, organizing, summarizing, and planning."
- "Ask a family AI question."

Carry the scope caveat where needed:

> Family AI help is a proposed/resource path until PJ approves a formal offer. Copy should present it as guidance, setup help, or a pilot-friendly inquiry path, not a mature packaged service.

Avoid phrases like:

- "Make AI safe for your kids."
- "Protect your family completely."
- "AI-proof your home."
- "Solve schoolwork with AI."
- "Let AI manage your household."
- "Certified child-safe AI."
- "Guaranteed privacy."
- "Use AI for every family decision."

## Sales Posture

The site should sell through usefulness. Prioritize:

- Guides.
- Checklists.
- Examples.
- Clear boundaries.
- "Bring one problem" entry points.
- Founder-led trust.

Use soft CTAs as defaults:

- "Start with one workflow"
- "Ask about your process"
- "Get practical AI help"
- "Map the first fix"
- "Ask a family AI question"
- "Start with safety"
- "Talk through your setup"

Use direct CTAs only where context is clearly commercial:

- "Request a workflow review"
- "Contact AIssisted Consulting"
- "Schedule a discovery call"

Avoid primary CTAs like:

- "Book now"
- "Buy now"
- "Claim your spot"
- "Scale instantly"
- "Automate everything"

## Trust And Safety Language

Required themes:

- AI supports people; it does not remove human responsibility.
- Privacy and control are design constraints, not afterthoughts.
- Sensitive data needs boundaries.
- Some workflows should not be automated.
- Unknown is better than fake certainty.
- Important decisions need human review.

Do not claim:

- Legal compliance.
- Security certification.
- Medical, financial, school-policy, or child-safety expertise.
- Guaranteed AI ranking or answer-engine placement.
- Guaranteed revenue, lead, booking, or conversion outcomes.
- Live AVOS test results unless evidence is later produced.
- Live SlipperyAPeI action support unless intentionally implemented and approved.

## ASEO Copy Rules

To support AVOS consistency:

1. Every important fact must appear in visible page copy before it appears in schema, JSON feeds, `llms.txt`, knowledge files, or `agent.json`.
2. Page copy, schema, feeds, `llms.txt`, and knowledge files must use the same business name, phone, email, founder, location, and service-area facts.
3. Proposed family guidance must be labeled conservatively anywhere it could be interpreted as a service offer.
4. Dynamic facts such as prices, timelines, guarantees, or package scope should have one canonical source or be omitted.
5. Do not create pages just for keywords. Each page needs a real visitor job and a preferred next action.

## Page-Level Tone Notes

### Home

Tone: warm, calm, helpful.

Job: split visitors into small-business help and family AI guidance without making the page feel like a sales funnel.

### Small Business Help

Tone: practical owner-to-owner help.

Job: make owners feel understood and show that the first step is one workflow, not a giant transformation.

### Family AI Help

Tone: protective, plain-language, non-alarmist.

Job: help families ask safer AI questions without implying certification or guaranteed safety.

### Services

Tone: concrete and scoped.

Job: explain what AIssisted can help build or configure, and where human review stays involved.

### Privacy And Control

Tone: serious but accessible.

Job: state boundaries clearly and help visitors trust that the work is not "AI at any cost."

### Contact

Tone: low pressure.

Job: ask for one workflow or one family AI question so the first reply can be useful.

## Forbidden Claim List

Do not write or imply:

- "Guaranteed AI visibility."
- "Guaranteed citations."
- "Guaranteed rankings."
- "Guaranteed revenue."
- "Guaranteed safety."
- "Compliant by default."
- "Replace staff."
- "Replace parents."
- "No human review needed."
- "Fully autonomous business."
- "Fully autonomous household."
- "We ran AVOS and proved improvement" unless a future evidence file supports it.
- "Agents can submit forms, book appointments, or complete transactions" unless SlipperyAPeI implementation and approval explicitly support it.

## Copy Review Checklist

Before any page copy is accepted:

- Does it help before it sells?
- Does it name a concrete workflow or family problem?
- Does it keep human judgment in charge?
- Does it avoid guarantees?
- Does it keep family AI guidance scoped conservatively?
- Does it match source-truth facts?
- Does it point to one clear next action?
- Does it avoid generic AI hype?
