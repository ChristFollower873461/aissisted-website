Service Architecture

Services scoped around operational outcomes, not feature checklists.

The rebuild candidate clarifies what we implement, how it is delivered, and where each service track connects to pricing, industries, and contact.

Track 1

Intake automation that captures requests consistently and routes the right next action.

Call and Form Intake

Normalize new requests into a structured intake record with urgency tier, service type, and required follow-up owner.

  • Missed-call and voicemail workflows.
  • Web form parsing with required context checks.
  • Escalation flags for emergency categories.

Lead Qualification Logic

Use owner-approved criteria to separate priority leads, prevent dead-end follow-up loops, and reduce response lag.

  • Business-specific qualification fields.
  • Response templates with clear next steps.
  • Hand-off triggers to staff when needed.

Track 2

Scheduling and dispatch support that aligns with how your team already operates.

Appointment Coordination

Automate booking windows, confirmation messages, and reschedule requests while preserving service-area and crew constraints.

  • Calendar window rules mapped to your coverage model.
  • Reminder sequences to reduce no-shows.
  • Intake context attached to booked jobs.

Field-Team Visibility

Daily summaries and dispatch context give staff and owners the same operational view.

  • Daily queue summaries grouped by urgency and route.
  • Status updates tied to customer communication.
  • Clear ownership between office and field roles.

Track 3

Follow-up and reporting systems that close loops and expose what is working.

Post-Service Follow-up

Trigger configurable sequences for check-ins, review requests, and recurring service reminders.

Owner Reporting

Generate daily and weekly summaries focused on response speed, booked jobs, and conversion progress.

Iteration Cadence

Use real exceptions and missed cases to tune workflow behavior without destabilizing day-to-day operations.
